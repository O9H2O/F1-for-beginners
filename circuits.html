Types of Circuit

Street Circuits

Temporary circuits built on public roads. Monaco, Baku, Singapore, Las Vegas, and Jeddah are street circuits. They're bumpy, narrow (no room for error), and typically produce dramatic races. There are no run-off areas — just walls.

Permanent Circuits

Purpose-built racing tracks like Silverstone, Monza, Suzuka, and Spa. These have proper gravel traps and tarmac run-off areas, and tend to have more consistent racing conditions.

Hybrid Circuits

Some venues like Melbourne's Albert Park and Montréal's Gilles Villeneuve are semi-permanent — using roads in a park or on an island, with temporary barriers added for race weekends.

Famous Circuits

Monaco

The most prestigious race on the calendar. Famously narrow streets through Monte Carlo, a tunnel section, and some of the most iconic corners in all of motorsport. Winning Monaco is the ultimate achievement for any racing driver.

Spa-Francorchamps

Widely considered the greatest racing circuit in the world. The legendary Eau Rouge / Raidillon sequence, the Pouhon corner, and the unpredictable Belgian weather make for almost always dramatic racing.

Monza

The "Temple of Speed." Italy's national circuit in a royal park near Milan. The fastest circuit on the calendar — cars reach 360 km/h down the straights. Always delivers spectacular racing due to its unique low-downforce setup requirements.

Suzuka

A figure-of-eight layout with the legendary 130R corner and the Esses section. Loved by drivers for its technical demands and by fans for its passionate Japanese crowd.
