Fun Facts

The numbers, records, and feats that make Formula 1 the most extraordinary sport on Earth.

⚡
0–100 km/h in Under 2 Seconds
A modern F1 car accelerates from 0 to 100 km/h (62 mph) in approximately 1.6 seconds. For comparison, a Bugatti Veyron takes about 2.5 seconds.
🔥
Cockpit Temperatures Hit 50°C
Inside the cockpit, drivers endure temperatures of up to 50°C (122°F) throughout a race. They lose up to 3 kg of body weight in sweat during a single race.
🧠
The Brain Works at 150+ km/h
At maximum cornering speeds, drivers experience forces of up to 6G — more than jet fighter pilots in a turn. Their neck muscles must support a head that effectively weighs 30 kg.
🏎️
The Car Is Lighter Than Two Passengers
A complete F1 car weighs just 798 kg — including the driver, fuel, and all fluids. That's lighter than many city cars with only two people in them.
🔧
Pit Stop: 1.8 Seconds
The world record for an F1 pit stop (four tyres changed) is 1.80 seconds, set by Red Bull Racing in 2023. Twenty mechanics work in perfectly choreographed precision.
🌡️
Brakes Glow at 1,000°C
Under heavy braking, F1 brake discs reach temperatures of around 1,000°C — hot enough to glow red-orange. The carbon fibre discs can be seen glowing from the grandstands at night races.
💨
Downforce Greater Than Car Weight
At high speed, an F1 car generates over 1,500 kg of aerodynamic downforce — nearly double the car's own weight. This is why they could theoretically drive upside down through a tunnel.
💰
An F1 Engine Costs $10 Million
A single power unit (engine + hybrid systems) costs approximately $10–15 million USD and is only allowed to last a maximum of three race weekends before it must be replaced — or the driver gets a grid penalty.
🎮
Steering Wheels Cost $100,000
An F1 steering wheel contains over 20 buttons, switches, and dials, and costs around $100,000 to manufacture. Drivers control everything from brake bias to differential settings to engine modes while travelling at 300 km/h.
👟
Tyres Last 40 Laps and Cost $4,000
A single set of F1 tyres costs about $4,000. Teams use an average of 13 sets per race weekend. Pirelli supplies over 1,800 sets of tyres to the entire field for a typical Grand Prix.
🔊
Teams Transmit 100GB of Data Per Race
Each car transmits around 100GB of data to the team during a race weekend via over 300 sensors monitoring every aspect of the car's performance in real time.
🏁
The Monaco Lap Record Is Under 1 Min 12 Sec
The Monaco street circuit is the shortest track on the calendar at 3.337 km. The lap record is 1:10.166 — meaning an average speed of around 171 km/h through some of the tightest racing streets in the world.
🌍
500 Million Viewers Watch F1
Formula 1 has an estimated cumulative TV audience of around 1.5 billion viewers across the season, with individual races attracting up to 100 million viewers globally.
🏆
The Most Dominant Season Ever: 2023
Max Verstappen won 19 of 22 races in the 2023 season — a win rate of 86.4%. The previous record was Michael Schumacher's 13 wins from 18 races in 2004 (72.2%). Both records were considered unbreakable before Verstappen broke them.
