Points System

How drivers and teams accumulate points on their way to the world championship.

Race Points

Points are awarded to the top 10 finishers in every Grand Prix. An extra point is available for the fastest lap — but only if the driver who sets it is in the top 10.

Drivers' Championship

Every point a driver scores in races and sprints throughout the season counts towards the Drivers' Championship. The driver with the most points at the end of the season is the World Drivers' Champion.

There are no drops (drivers count all their scores). If two drivers tie on points, the tiebreaker is whichever driver has more wins — then more second places, and so on.

Constructors' Championship

The Constructors' Championship is contested by teams (constructors). A team's score is the combined total of both their drivers' points in every race.

This matters enormously: prize money, prestige, and the team's position in the pit lane allocation are all based on Constructors' Championship standing. Teams lose millions of dollars in prize money for every position they drop in the standings.

Why does the gap matter?

The gap between first and second place is 7 points (25 vs 18). This means a driver can overcome a 7-point deficit in a single race by winning while their rival finishes second.

With 24 races in a season, the maximum possible score is 25 × 24 + 1 (fastest laps) = 601 points — though no driver has ever come close to that.

In 2023, Max Verstappen won 19 of 22 races for a total of 575 points — one of the most dominant championship performances in history.
